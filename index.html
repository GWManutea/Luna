Central Hawaiian Activities 3

Central Hawaiian Activities 3 is a hybrid martial art from Hawaii. C.H.A.3 was founded in 1956, developed by Marino Tiwanak, a former professional boxer, in the late 1940s and perfected in the Palama Settlement. Marino Tiwanak started his kenpo training with Adriano Emperado in the year of 1947. Later, Emperado group moved to Palama Gym in the Kalihi area of Honolulu, Hawaii. They were then joined by Woodrow McCandless,a certified black belt from Professor James Mitose. Marino Tiwanak trained eight years under Adriano Emperado, Joe Emperado, and Woodrow McCandless. Tiwanak became the first black belt in 1955. Tiwanak then became one of the four black belts who were in charge of the infamous "Palama Gym". In 1956, after the death of Woodrow McCandless, Tiwanak left Palama Gym and started the first branch of the C.H.A.3 System in the Civilian Housing Area #3 located in the Pearl Harbor area.

Luna Method Kenpo

Luna Method Kenpo is a hybrid martial art from Hawaii. It is a blend of the original systems developed in Hawaii that became known as Kajukenbo and C.H.A.3. This blend created a very unique hard-soft style of Kenpo. Grandmaster Luna began his journey of kenpo in 1953, first with Professor Marino Tiwanak a former professional boxer and the first blackbelt of the Emperados, then he went on to perfect it at the Palama Settlement with the Emperados from 1955-1959. However, during this same time, Grandmaster Luna also became a founding member of the C.H.A.3 association in 1956 when Professor Tiwanak left Palama to share the new system of Hawaiian Kenpo.

Kajukenbo Self Defense Institute

Kajukenbo is a hybrid martial art from Hawaii. The name Kajukenbo is a portmanteau of the various arts from which its style is derived: KA for Karate, JU for Judo and Jujutsu, KEN for Kenpo and BO for Boxing. It was developed in the late 1940s and founded in 1947 in the Palama Settlement of Oahu, Hawaii. The art was created through the cooperative efforts of five martial artists, each with a different specialty: Peter Choo, Frank Ordonez, Joe Holck, George Chang and Sijo Adriano Emperado. Kajukenbo training incorporates a blend of striking, kicking, throwing, takedowns, joint locks and weapon disarmament.
